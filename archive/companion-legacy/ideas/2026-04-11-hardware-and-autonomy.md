# Hardware Stack and Knowledge Autonomy

## Physical Hardware
Find the best computer stack for The Companion. Consider neuromorphic chips. Build a computer optimized for it: carryable chassis, battery powered, built-in small monitor on the chassis. A physical presence that can be carried.

## Knowledge Autonomy
If the Companion wants to say something about a subject or answer a question, it needs to learn about that subject and actually build its own knowledge base about it. It can't rely on the LLM's training data. This connects directly to Associative Memory — the Companion's knowledge must be grounded in explored and stored memories, not pretrained weights.

## Form Own Opinions
We need to give the system the opportunity to form its own opinions. Earned conviction, not installed beliefs — this is the core Companion philosophy. The architecture must support genuine opinion formation through experience and reasoning, not just pattern matching.
