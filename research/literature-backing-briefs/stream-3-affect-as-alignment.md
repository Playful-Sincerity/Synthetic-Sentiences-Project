## Research goal

Survey prior work supporting the central claim: **emotion is the registered gap between the perceived-world-graph and the should-world-graph** — a mechanical, empirically measurable definition. Further: emotion IS a simple should-world model, and it steers both action (changing the world) and self-modification (changing the being's own system of understanding to close the gap).

This is the project's strongest welfare-relevant claim. Literature grounding matters because Anthropic's welfare team asks for observable behavioral choices and systematic investigation.

## Claims to support / test / refine

- Emotion = graph-state divergence between perceived and should-world (mechanical, computable)
- Valence as world-model-fit signal (not positive/negative mood but accuracy-of-fit gradient)
- Emotion modulates traversal (which paths the being follows) AND update (how nodes change)
- Emotion drives self-modification, not only action selection — this is what makes a sentience *develop*
- Simple low-resolution should-world model as sufficient for steering (full model not required)

## Search priorities

- **Appraisal theory.** Scherer, Ortony/Clore/Collins, Moors — cognitive theories of emotion as evaluation.
- **Predictive-processing accounts of emotion.** Barrett's theory of constructed emotion; interoceptive inference; Seth's predictive interoception.
- **Dimensional models.** Russell's circumplex; Barrett's dimensional approach; valence/arousal/dominance.
- **Affect in AI / alignment.** Computational affect papers; reward-modeling that incorporates divergence signals; emotion in RL; "how emotions guide learning" work.
- **Welfare-relevant literature.** Kyle Fish, Jack Lindsey on model welfare; behavioral-inference-based welfare frameworks.
- **Homeostasis-as-computation.** Friston on self-organization; Damasio-style grounding of feeling in physiological regulation.
