## 17:05 — [Insight]

**What:** Closing observations from the session that weren't surfaced in any single priority entry. Four worth recording.

1. **Naming-collision discovery.** The old diagram and the old SPEC carried Mirror/Trees/Matrix as labels for GRP-internal blocks (Imagination / Dual Perception / World Model). After the SSP unification, those exact names refer to *different* things at the project level: the Mirror subsystem (cross-session self-observation), the Trees subsystem (working memory), and MWM (the sibling repo). Keeping the old labels would have produced a real ambiguity — a reader looking at the diagram could not tell whether "Mirror" meant the imagination engine or the persistent observer. The rewrite resolved this by using neutral mechanism names (IMAGINATION ENGINE / DUAL PERCEPTION / MWM-as-sibling-repo) and then adding an explicit SSP SUBSYSTEMS cluster showing where the GRP-internal blocks integrate with each subsystem-by-name. This is a generalizable lesson for the rest of SSP: when an internal block borrows a name that the umbrella project also uses for a different thing, the borrowing must be explicit, or it must stop.

2. **The unification thesis is the through-line.** End-state imagination in GRP, the should-world-model in Values, and dream-cycle simulation in Cycles are the same mechanism applied at three scales — task-scale, being-scale, moment-scale. This is now stated in SPEC § Imagine-Before-Act, in the paper-section.md ¶4, and is implicit in the diagram's edges from the imagination engine into Cycles. Worth promoting upward: this is the kind of cross-subsystem unification the SSP paper benefits from making visible at the program level, not just in the perception subsection.

3. **Q5 is paper-blocking.** The Generative Collaborative reframe is recorded as a contribution in SPEC, README, paper-section, and the diagram. But until there is a formal mathematical statement — single shared loss, or two losses provably aligned in expectation — the reframe is *framing*, not *architecture*. A paper that lists it as a contribution without that grounding will be flagged in review. The honest move is either to ground it before shipping, or to demote it from "contribution" to "framing decision" in the paper. Worth deciding before draft time.

4. **The factoring decision worth naming explicitly.** GRP does not own a world-model schema. It writes content into MWM's primitives. This was implicit in the old SPEC (the "Matrix" block was AM/MWM-derived) but the rewrite makes it structural: `world-model/` in the module map became `mwm-bridge/`, the data-structure section ends with a "MWM-side nodes/edges produced by GRP" block describing *content* not *schema*, and the architecture diagram shows MWM as a separate cluster outside the cognitive core. This is the same factoring move the SSP architecture relies on across all subsystems — the graph is the common substrate, every subsystem is a specific traversal pattern over it. Recording so future-me doesn't re-litigate.

**Why:** Per-priority entries captured what changed in each file. These four observations are about what *the work as a whole* implies — naming hygiene becoming a real thing as SSP unifies, the imagination-mechanism unification surfacing as a stronger thesis than originally framed, the GCN claim's actual epistemic status being weaker than the prose makes it sound, and the deliberate factoring that the rewrite codifies. None of these belongs to one priority; all of them are load-bearing for future sessions.

**Means:** Future GRP work should (a) check Q5 before shipping the GCN reframe as a contribution, (b) consider whether the imagination-mechanism unification should be promoted to a program-level claim in the SSP paper rather than tucked inside the perception subsection, and (c) treat MWM-as-substrate / GRP-as-perception-subsystem as the canonical factoring (no world-model schema living inside perception/, ever). Also: a parallel paper-side commit (`260a438` paper: reframe around real synthetic sentience) landed on `paper/SSP-proposal.md` during this session; no conflict because scopes were separate, but worth noting that GRP-side and paper-side edits ran concurrently.
