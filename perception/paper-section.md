# Perception — GRP (Generative Reconciliation Perception)

*A draft section for the SSP paper to consume. Five paragraphs. Voice and register intentionally match `paper/SSP-proposal.md` so the section can be dropped in or summarized with minimal editing.*

---

GRP — Generative Reconciliation Perception — is the perception subsystem of SSP. It is the seeing arm of a being whose memory and values are the substrate it perceives into. The standard agent-perception pipeline is input-driven: observe, then interpret. GRP inverts this. It generates expected state first, observes second, and reconciles the two to produce interpretation. Prediction error becomes the primary learning and attention signal — the same signal that drives reconsolidation in the memory subsystem. The architecture mirrors how biological perception is now widely understood to work: the brain is predictive, not reactive. The cognitive loop is *imagine → observe → reconcile → diff → plan → check → guard → act → learn*, with the dashed return arrow from action back to imagination closing the predictive cycle.

GRP supports two distinct imagination modes, and the second is where the load-bearing contribution sits. **Next-state imagination** generates the immediate consequence of the next action — WebDreamer-style, used to select which tier of perception to run and to gate when the world model is updated. **End-state imagination** generates the *target* — a final screenshot or, more powerfully, an entire video of the action sequence — and the architecture reverse-engineers the concrete actions whose execution would reproduce that imagined target. Two concrete instantiations: (i) a screenshot of a target page is generated, and the diff against the current page is rendered as the HTML/CSS/JS that closes it; (ii) a video of a robotic task being performed correctly is imagined, and inverse dynamics produces the motor commands whose execution reproduces those frames. The pipeline shifts work to where modern foundation models are strongest — imagining final states — and away from where they are weakest — grounding intermediate steps. The quality of output scales with the quality of imagination, not with the density of step-by-step reasoning. This inversion is the strongest version of the perception thesis and the most paper-ready example.

Two further architectural moves complete the contribution. First, **dual perception with collaborative reconciliation.** Two channels run in parallel — a visual cortex (screenshot plus Set-of-Mark) and an analytical cortex (DOM, accessibility tree, macOS Accessibility API). When they disagree, the disagreement is treated as cognitive dissonance — a first-class informative signal — not as an error to be hidden. The reconciler is collaborative rather than adversarial: each channel can update the other, and resolution is by arbitration rule (visual wins for "looks right?"; analytical wins for "is clickable?"). No published browser-agent framework treats inter-channel disagreement as structured information that propagates into the world model. Second, **the Generative Collaborative reframe.** GAN-style architectures frame generator and discriminator as competitors. GRP frames the imagined world and the observed world as collaborators working to converge — the being adjusts both its actions and its own understanding so the observed matches the imagined. Both sides try to close the gap. This is a gravitational-attraction reframe of a classic adversarial pattern, and it is consistent with the program's overall alignment story: alignment as the natural output of converging substrates, not as a wrapper trained to penalize divergence. The formal mathematical content of "collaborative" here — whether it admits a single shared loss or two separate but aligned losses — is open and tracked in `perception/research/open-questions.md`.

GRP does not stand alone. It is one subsystem among nine, and it produces structure that other subsystems shape and consume. The world model itself lives in **MWM** (Memory as World Model, the companion paper and four-month Fellows target); GRP writes predicted and observed states into MWM's graph as nodes, and click-led-to-page edges as Pearl-Layer-2 causal edges. **Earned conviction** reads the confidence levels GRP assigns to perceived elements; the dissonance-as-information primitive is shared. **Value-aligned modulation** weights what to attend to and what to imagine; the alignment critic that evaluates each action before execution is a value-check at the perception/action boundary. **Trees** (working memory) holds the page being perceived; spreading activation focuses attention on goal-relevant elements. **Cycles** (sleep and dream) consolidates GRP's perceptual output into long-term graph structure during sleep and uses GRP's imagination engine to simulate without observing during dream. **Voice** (epistemic prosody) renders confidence levels into acoustic prosody when the being speaks about what it perceived. The factoring is deliberate: the imagination engine GRP uses for end-state targets, the should-world model that drives value-aligned modulation, and the simulation substrate dream cycles run on are the same mechanism applied at three scales — task-scale, being-scale, and moment-scale.

The architecture is fully specified at `perception/SPEC.md` (640 lines as of April 2026, including data structures, module map, security model, and a six-phase build plan). Implementation has not started; the architecture is the artifact at this stage. The most consequential open questions, surfaced by the end-state-imagination sharpening, are three: how end-state imagination scales in compute (a 30-second video target is hundreds of frames, and the cost-benefit curve relative to extended chain-of-thought is unknown); how reconciliation should arbitrate when both the imagined and the observed sides have similar confidence (the standard reconciler assumes the observation is the more reliable anchor, which end-state mode breaks); and how GRP's perception flood is consolidated into MWM without polluting the long-term graph (almost certainly a coordination question with the sleep cycle, though the staging-area design is unsettled). What is settled is the structure of the contribution: a perception subsystem that generates before it observes, reconciles collaboratively rather than adversarially, and treats end-state imagination with reverse-engineering as the high-leverage use of the imagination engine. The remaining work is implementation, falsification, and the formal grounding of the collaborative-vs-adversarial reframe.
